About me

I am currently a data science associate at New York Genome Center in Dr. David Knowles group since 2021. I have been part of the Alzheimer's Disease Sequencing Project (ADSP) cohort funded by NIH ever since.

Before this, I received my Master's in bioinformatics and genomics at Penn State University in 2021, supervised by Dr. Yifei Huang .

My undergraduate major is in biotechnology and bio-industrial science , so surprise surprise, I did 2 years of animal experiments studying how lung cancer Cells influence macrophage (a type of while blood cells), while taking business courses from the School of management.

Improving cross-ancestral PRS transferability in Alzheimer’s Disease.

My first project in Knowles Lab, collaborate with Raj Lab at Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai

  • Use functional annotations created by several ML methods ( Enformer, DeepSea, Roadmap, and some tissue-specific transcription factors for functional fine-mapping.
  • Calculate Polygenic Risk Score and compare the proformance between different ethnic groups (European, Hispanic, and African American)
  • Preprint in progress! Meanwhile, you can check the latest poster
    • I presented in ASHG

Predicting RNA expression in Alzheimer’s Disease using deep learning based TWAS.

My second project in Knowles Lab, collaborate with Raj Lab at Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai

  • Use Enformer to predict RNA expression in monocyte.
  • Calculate score for every gene in every individual.
  • Validate the prediction using real paitient RNA-seq from Raj Lab

Variant prioritization and evaluation in Mendelian disorders

My work during my Master's in Huang Lab.

  • Build a inheritance-mode-aware supervised learning classifier (logistic regression, SVM, gradient boosting tree) for variant effect prediction.
  • Included 14 well-used annotations in this model.
  • More details are in my Master's Thesis .
